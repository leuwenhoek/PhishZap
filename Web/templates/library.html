CRITICAL

Phishing 101

The most common form of social engineering. Attackers use spoofed emails and websites to steal credentials.

PREVENTION: * Always check the domain name.
* Use PhishZap URL Checker.
* Enable Multi-Factor Auth (MFA).
MEDIUM

Smishing (SMS)

Fraudulent text messages designed to induce people to reveal personal info or click malicious mobile links.

PREVENTION: * Never reply to "Bank" SMS.
* Use PhishZap Message Checker.
* Block unknown international senders.
CRITICAL

Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts your files. The attacker demands a ransom to restore access.

PREVENTION: * Daily offline backups.
* Never open .exe from emails.
* Keep OS & Browser updated.
LOW

Social Scraping

Automated collection of your personal data from social media to build better phishing profiles.

PREVENTION: * Set profiles to Private.
* Audit third-party app access.
* Don't share location live.
MEDIUM

Juice Jacking

A type of cyberattack involving a charging port that doubles as a data connection, typically over USB.

PREVENTION: * Use your own wall charger.
* Use a "USB Condom" blocker.
* Avoid public USB hubs.
